Visualizing digital influence

A conceptual poster series about attention, algorithms and the hidden systems behind social media. Using interconnected visuals and a futuristic aesthetic, the project explores how digital platforms influence behaviour while making users feel in control of their own experience.

The project began with research into social media addiction, algorithmic influence and online echo chambers. Through moodboards, sketches and visual exploration, I searched for a visual language that could translate these invisible systems into something tangible.

The final concept revolves around four interconnected elements: a hand representing control, a smartphone representing consumption, a brain representing perception and a pair of scissors symbolizing the possibility of breaking the cycle. Connected through threads, the elements form a visual metaphor for the dependencies created by modern digital platforms.
