Motion Studies

Where modelling ends and performance begins. These are character animation studies — the rigging, weight and timing that turn a static 3D model into something that feels alive.

Loop & turntable studies

Movement,
not just models

A character only becomes believable once it moves. After modelling, I build a rig — a digital skeleton — and use it to test poses, cycles and the way weight shifts through a body.

These studies are where I refine timing and appeal: the small anticipation before a step, the follow-through in cloth and hair, the pauses that give a performance its rhythm.

  • Rigging characters for clean, expressive deformation
  • Pose-to-pose and looped animation studies
  • Turntables to read silhouette and form in motion
